Disaster Response

I participated in the Coalition Warrior Interoperability Demonstration (CWID). We simulated disasters to test communication methods between emergency operations agencies.

Our Java application took hazardous materials sensor data and distributed it using the Common Alerting Protocol (CAP). The data was also presented on a web interface. My main development task was integrating Twitter and Facebook functionality into our existing application.

Over CWID's 3-week span, I demonstrated our software to people from a number of agencies, including officers from all U.S. military branches.

During the demonstration, we recorded findings which I used to wireframe a redesign of our mobile app.

Mobile Game Concept Sketches

I keep a sketchbook with me as often as possible to draw out ideas or simply to write descriptions of ideas. These are two samples.

One is a target practice game. You'd tap targets in numbered order under a certain amount of time. Different types of targets would have different properties so it could take a certain amount of taps to destroy, timed taps, etc.

The other is a concept inspired by those simple helicopter or jetpack games where your vehicle is constantly falling and you click to move it upwards. You time the clicks to navigate past obstacles. This is similar where you'd tap to keep your player from crashing into the floor.

The added element is that you'd tap with your other thumb to switch between the sketched world and the "real" world and would need to switch to one of the worlds to avoid obstacles in the other.

Windows 7 Group Chat Concept Mockup and Task Flow

I was a heavy Blackberry Group Chat user. Like, basically my life. Before Windows Phone was released, Microsoft provided some templates for developers and designers to use to plan for Windows Phone app development.

My friends and I knew our time with BBM was coming to an end. We held on until we made a mass exodus to the 4S using a mix of iMessage and GroupMe. But not before I gave the templates a whirl to see what group chat might look like on a Windows Phone.
